College of Lockpicking returns to Hack Manhattan!

  • October 4, 2012
  • Events / Uncategorized
  • Guan
  • Leave a comment
  • 0

You can pick your friends.
You can pick your beer.
But can you pick… a Lock?

Join us on Monday, October 15th, from 7-9pm as we embark on an ancient mechanical adventure to explore the inner workings of a modern lock, first developed by the Mesopotamians and Egyptians over 4000 years ago. Led by lockpicker Eric Michaud, this workshop will cover – soup to nuts – everything you need to know about how common locks work… and how to pick them open.

Whether you’re a complete novice curious to find out what pin-tumbler really means, a homeowner protecting your family’s treasures, or a veteran lockpicker with questions about advanced locks and techniques, this event is for you! With 2 solid hours of history, mechanical diagrams (“lock pr0n”), and hands-on lockpicking instruction… first we’ll open your minds, and then we’ll open some locks!

To properly learn this ancient art, you will need the proper tools. By registering for a ticket with a lockpick set, this guarantees you a seat at the workshop, and reserves – all for you – a brand-new 10-piece custom lockpick set made entirely in the USA, hand-picked by College of Lockpicking faculty to maximize utility for beginners and locksport enthusiasts alike, and offered for only $30.

If you already have some picks you’re itching to practice with, you can bring them along and your workshop cost is only $70!

BONUS: Hack Manhattan members get $20 off!! (Check your secret decoder ring for the discount code.)

EXTRA BONUS: 25% of the proceeds from the workshop fees will be donated to Hack Manhattan!

See ya there!

– Eric.

P.S. Visit the College of Lockpicking School Store to pick up custom lockpicking accessories and limited edition goodies as they become available! And don’t forget to Like our Facebook page andfollow @lockpickcollege on Twitter, to get updates about this and future Lockpicking events across the country and around the world!
