Space! (the infinite mostly-vacuum kind, not our new digs)

  • August 9, 2012
  • Uncategorized
  • Dave
  • Leave a comment
  • 0

On Saturday 18th August, we’re teaming up with the NYPL to host a public lecture and interactive event with a special guest, Frank O’Brien, who will demonstrate the inner workings of the Apollo Guidance Computer, one of the first digital multitasking computers, and a crucial element in getting people to the Moon in the 1960’s and 1970’s.

You can read more about the event (to be held at Jefferson Market Branch Library) and get your free tickets at:

http://apolloguidancecomputer.eventbrite.com

Please join us for what promises to be a fascinating afternoon.
